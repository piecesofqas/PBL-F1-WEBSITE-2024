Blueberry Pancakes

- Use whole wheat flour: Make healthy pancakes using whole wheat flour, oats, mashed banana, milk, eggs, and spices.
- Reduce added sugar: Make pancakes without added sugar.
- Use yogurt: Use regular or Greek yogurt, plain, vanilla, blueberry, or honey-flavored.
- Substitute sweeteners: Substitute honey, coconut sugar, or brown sugar for maple syrup.

Brown Rice Tofu

- Use firm tofu: Ensure it holds its shape when cooked.
- Marinate tofu: Marinate tofu cubes in soy sauce, paprika, garlic powder, and olive oil for flavor.
- Steam broccoli: Steam broccoli florets to keep nutrients intact.
- Use brown rice: For added fiber and nutrients, or substitute with white rice for a milder taste.

Caesar Salad

- Use fresh lettuce: Use crisp romaine lettuce as the base.
- Add whole-grain croutons: Swap traditional croutons for whole-grain or baked ones for a healthier crunch.
- Use a light dressing: Opt for a low-fat or homemade Caesar dressing to reduce calories.
- Incorporate protein: Add grilled chicken, shrimp, or chickpeas to make it more filling.

Face Wash

Wash your face twice daily with a gentle cleanser to remove dirt, oil, and makeup. Overwashing or using harsh products can strip natural oils and cause irritation.

moisturizer

Use a moisturizer suited to your skin type like for example, lightweight gel for oily skin, cream for dry skin to keep your skin hydrated and maintain its barrier function..

Sunscreen

Protect your skin from UV damage by using a broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30, even on cloudy days or indoors near windows
